# Germany VAT (Umsatzsteuer) Compliance for Invoices

## German Invoice Requirements (UStG §14)

### Mandatory Invoice Elements

According to German VAT law (Umsatzsteuergesetz), all invoices must contain:

1. **Vollständiger Name und Anschrift** (Complete name and address)
   - Supplier's full company name
   - Complete address including street, postal code, city
   
2. **Steuernummer oder USt-IdNr** (Tax number or VAT ID)
   - Tax number (Steuernummer) OR
   - VAT identification number (USt-IdNr)
   - Format: DE followed by 9 digits (e.g., DE123456789)

3. **Rechnungsdatum** (Invoice date)
   - Date when invoice is issued
   - Format: DD.MM.YYYY preferred

4. **Rechnungsnummer** (Invoice number)
   - Unique, sequential numbering
   - No gaps in sequence allowed
   - One-time use only

5. **Leistungsdatum** (Date of service/delivery)
   - When goods delivered or service completed
   - Can be period: "01.10.2025 - 31.10.2025"

6. **Leistungsbeschreibung** (Description of goods/services)
   - Clear, detailed description
   - Quantity and type of goods
   - Nature and scope of services

7. **Entgelt** (Consideration)
   - Net amount (before VAT)
   - Unit price if applicable
   - Any discounts or rebates

8. **Steuersatz** (Tax rate)
   - Applicable VAT rate clearly stated
   - 19% (Regelsteuersatz - standard rate)
   - 7% (ermäßigter Steuersatz - reduced rate)

9. **Steuerbetrag** (Tax amount)
   - VAT amount in Euros
   - Must be calculable from rate and net amount

10. **Gesamtbetrag** (Total amount)
    - Gross amount including VAT

## German VAT Rates

### Standard Rate (Regelsteuersatz): 19%
Applies to most goods and services:
- Electronics and appliances
- Clothing and textiles  
- Restaurant services
- Professional services
- Hotel accommodation
- Most imports

### Reduced Rate (ermäßigter Steuersatz): 7%
Applies to:
- Food and beverages (excluding restaurant)
- Books, newspapers, magazines
- Public transport
- Cultural events (theater, concerts)
- Agricultural products
- Hotel accommodation (without breakfast)
- Artworks
- Medical equipment

### Zero-Rated (0%): Exports
- Intra-EU supplies with valid VAT ID
- International transport
- Goods exported outside EU

### Tax-Exempt (steuerfrei)
No VAT charged for:
- Medical services
- Educational services
- Financial services
- Insurance services
- Rental of residential property

## Kleinunternehmerregelung (Small Business Rule)

### Threshold: €22,000
- Previous year revenue < €22,000
- Current year estimated < €50,000
- Can voluntarily opt out of exemption

### Requirements
Must state on invoice:
"Gemäß §19 UStG wird keine Umsatzsteuer berechnet"
(According to §19 UStG, no VAT is charged)

## Special Invoice Types

### Kleinbetragsrechnung (Small Amount Invoice)
**For amounts up to €250 (including VAT):**

Simplified requirements:
- Supplier name and address
- Invoice date
- Description of goods/services
- Total amount including VAT
- Applicable tax rate

No need for:
- Invoice number
- Customer name
- Separate display of tax amount

### Gutschrift (Credit Note / Self-Billing)
- Customer creates invoice for supplier
- Requires prior agreement
- Must include: "Gutschrift" designation
- All standard invoice elements required

### Reverse Charge (Umkehr der Steuerschuldnerschaft)
For B2B services from abroad:
- No German VAT charged by supplier
- Recipient becomes tax debtor
- Must state: "Steuerschuldnerschaft des Leistungsempfängers"

## Electronic Invoicing (E-Rechnung)

### From January 1, 2025: Mandatory for B2B
- XRechnung or ZUGFeRD format required
- Hybrid format acceptable
- Structured data (XML) essential
- PDF alone no longer sufficient for B2B

### Requirements
- Authenticated format
- Integrity protection
- Machine-readable
- Long-term readable
- Archived according to GoBD

## GoBD Compliance (Accounting Principles)

### Storage Requirements
- Invoices must be stored for 10 years
- Original format required
- Tamper-proof storage
- Accessible for tax audit
- Indexed and searchable

### Digital Archiving
- Must preserve original format
- Z1/Z3 certification recommended
- Proper backup procedures
- Access rights management
- Audit trail mandatory

## Cross-Border Transactions

### Intra-EU B2B Sales (Innergemeinschaftliche Lieferung)
**Requirements:**
- Valid VAT ID of customer required
- VAT ID verification mandatory (VIES check)
- Zero-rated (0% VAT)
- Documentation: delivery proof, transport documents
- Recapitulative statement (ZM) filing required
- Invoice must state: "Steuerfreie innergemeinschaftliche Lieferung"

### Intra-EU B2C Sales
- German VAT applies if threshold not exceeded
- Threshold: €10,000 across all EU
- Above threshold: destination country VAT

### Non-EU Exports
- Zero-rated (0% VAT)
- Export documentation required
- Customs declaration
- Proof of export

### Imports from Non-EU
- Import VAT due at customs
- 19% or 7% depending on goods
- Can be reclaimed if VAT registered

## Fraud Prevention

### Red Flags for German Tax Authorities
- Missing or invalid VAT ID (USt-IdNr)
- Non-sequential invoice numbers
- Backdated invoices
- Incorrect VAT rates
- Missing mandatory information
- Invoices from Briefkastenfirmen (shell companies)
- Unusual payment patterns
- Circular transactions

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Administrative Fines
- Missing invoice elements: €100 - €5,000 per invoice
- Incorrect VAT amount: Correction + interest
- Late VAT returns: 1% per month penalty

### Tax Evasion (Steuerhinterziehung)
- Criminal offense in Germany
- Imprisonment up to 5 years
- Fines up to €50,000 or more
- Tax liability remains

### VAT Deduction Consequences
- Incorrect invoice = no input VAT deduction
- Buyer loses right to reclaim VAT
- Can be corrected if amended within deadline

## Industry-Specific Rules

### Construction Industry (Baugewerbe)
- §13b UStG reverse charge mechanism
- Building services follow special rules
- Proof of USt-IdNr essential
- Liability for unpaid VAT possible

### Scrap Metal / Mobile Phones
- Reverse charge applies
- High fraud risk sector
- Enhanced documentation required

### Differentiation Tax (Differenzbesteuerung)
- Used goods, art, antiques
- Special margin scheme
- VAT only on dealer's margin
- Invoice must state margin scheme used

## Best Practices

1. **Always verify VAT IDs** via VIES before zero-rating
2. **Sequential numbering** without gaps or duplicates
3. **Clear separation** of net amount, VAT, gross amount
4. **Timely issuance** within 6 months of service
5. **Proper archiving** according to GoBD
6. **Regular updates** on VAT rate changes
7. **Staff training** on invoice requirements
8. **System validation** for automatic invoicing
9. **Quarterly reviews** of compliance
10. **Professional advice** for complex cases

## Recent Changes (2024-2025)

### E-Invoice Mandate
- Mandatory for B2B from January 1, 2025
- Transition period until 2027
- Paper invoices phased out for B2B
- B2C still accepts traditional formats

### VAT in the Digital Age (ViDA)
- Enhanced reporting requirements
- Real-time VAT reporting planned
- Platform liability rules
- Stricter enforcement

### Energy Crisis Special Rules
- Temporary VAT reductions expired
- Gas and heating back to 19%
- Monitor for future changes

## Common Mistakes

1. **Wrong VAT rate** (7% vs 19%)
2. **Missing Leistungsdatum** (date of supply)
3. **Invalid USt-IdNr format**
4. **Non-sequential invoice numbers**
5. **Mixing net and gross amounts**
6. **Incomplete address information**
7. **Missing reverse charge statement**
8. **Incorrect Kleinunternehmer statement**
9. **No GoBD-compliant archiving**
10. **Paper invoices for B2B (post-2025)**
